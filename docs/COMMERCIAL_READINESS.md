# Commercial readiness

This project is open-source under the MIT license. That covers the
**code**. It does **not** cover the **data** the code calls out to,
nor does it grant any professional advice. Read this page before
deploying the dashboard commercially or to a public audience beyond a
demo.

---

## What this is, and is not

**This software is:**

- A research and educational dashboard.
- A transparent, rule-based scoring tool.
- A deterministic voyage-economics calculator (VLCC Route Lab).
- An open-source codebase you can fork, audit, and extend.

**This software is NOT:**

- **Investment advice.** Equity signals are unvalidated heuristics.
- **Routing advice.** The Route Lab does not consider weather, traffic
  separation schemes, port slot availability, charterer instructions,
  or current threat reports.
- **Insurance advice.** AWRP percentages are user-editable
  placeholders. Real underwriter quotes vary by hull, voyage, and
  current Joint War Committee listings.
- **Legal advice.** EU ETS scope rules are implemented per public EC /
  EMSA documents at the time of writing; verify with counsel before
  acting on regulatory inputs.
- **Warranty-backed.** Data may be delayed, missing, or wrong. Use at
  your own risk.

## Data-source licensing

Every external feed has its own licence. **You** are responsible for
verifying that your deployment respects them.

| Source | Used for | Licence / TOS implications |
|--------|----------|----------------------------|
| **yfinance** (Yahoo Finance) | Prices, fundamentals, fallback news | Yahoo's TOS govern automated access. The `yfinance` library is unofficial; reliability and continued availability are not guaranteed. Commercial redistribution of Yahoo data may require a paid licence. |
| **NewsAPI** (newsapi.org) | Primary news in live mode | Free tier is **for development environments only** and is rate-limited. **Production / public deployments must use a paid plan or substitute another news provider.** Headlines are summaries; underlying articles remain copyright of their publishers. |
| **BDRY ETF** (Yahoo) | Public proxy for the Baltic Dry Index | The ETF is a publicly traded security whose prices come from yfinance. It is **not** the Baltic Dry Index. The actual BDI requires a Baltic Exchange licence. |
| **Sample data** (`sample_data/`) | Demo mode | Synthetic, deterministic, generated by `sample_data/generate.py`. No copyrighted content. |
| **TCE rates / FVG / vessel valuations / AIS / freight indices** | Not implemented | Require paid licensed providers (Baltic Exchange, Clarksons, VesselsValue, Kpler, MarineTraffic). The Data Health tab lists these as "not configured" until you supply keys and integrate them. |

If you publish a hosted instance, check the licence of every active
data source against your deployment's audience size and revenue model.
Free tiers are usually for dev/personal use; commercial redistribution
typically requires a paid plan and explicit attribution.

## Recommended public-deployment defaults

- **Default to demo mode** unless your commercial rights to live data
  are clear. Demo mode uses bundled synthetic fixtures only — no
  network calls, no quota, no licensing risk.
- **Display the disclaimers** that already appear in the dashboard
  footer and in the Route Lab's Assumptions & Sources tab. Don't strip
  them.
- **Never expose user secrets to the browser.** Streamlit runs
  server-side; secrets stay in the host environment. Don't add
  client-side analytics that surface env vars.
- **Document your live data feeds** alongside the deployment, including
  any commercial licences you hold.

## Privacy

- The application does not intentionally collect user data, telemetry,
  cookies, or analytics. Streamlit usage stats are **off** by default
  in `.streamlit/config.toml` and the Docker image.
- The hosting platform (Streamlit Community Cloud, Hugging Face
  Spaces, your own VPS, Kubernetes ingress, CDN) may add request logs
  or analytics independently. Document those for your users.
- The Route Lab and Watchlist do not transmit user inputs to any
  third party. Editable scenarios stay in the running Streamlit
  session.

---

## Logging hygiene

- The dashboard sanitises provider error messages before display
  (URLs containing `apiKey=`, `Authorization=`, etc. are redacted).
- The terminal smoke tests do not print secrets (verified by the
  CI / local secret-leak grep).
- If you add custom logging, follow the same rule: never log full
  request URLs, request headers, or env-var values. Redact at the
  source.

## Pre-commercial checklist

Before promoting a hosted instance from "demo" to "production":

1. Confirm rotation of any previously exposed secret.
2. Verify your live data providers' commercial licences and quotas.
3. Replace the NewsAPI free tier with a paid plan or alternate
   provider (and update `providers.py` if needed).
4. Wire any paid maritime feeds you intend to surface (Baltic
   Exchange, Clarksons, etc.) — they are placeholders today.
5. Audit the dashboard for any wording that could read as advice;
   the existing disclaimers are deliberately blunt — keep them.
6. Add your own privacy / terms-of-service page if your audience
   warrants one.
7. Run `python3 scripts/security_check.py` and `python3 scripts/doctor.py`
   from a clean clone before each release.
